# Hackathon Guide

<img src="/images/workshops/her-blog-back-to-school-jetson-dev-kit-1080x1080.png" alt="NVIDIA Jetson Developer Kit" width="360" style="float: right; margin-left: 20px; margin-bottom: 20px;" />

The NVIDIA Jetson empowers you to bring your innovative ideas to life.

It's a powerful, compact edge AI computer with plenty of examples and learning resources to help your team build an impressive AI project at a hackathon. See [examples of successful hackathon projects](/community?tag=Hackathon).

Explore the tips, guides, and resources below to get started and make the most of your Jetson experience.

Good luck, have fun, and happy hacking!

## Physical Setup

<Tabs labels={["Headless setup (Recommended)", "Monitor-attached setup"]}>
  <div class="nv-tab-panel active">
    <p>A <strong>headless setup</strong> with Jetson's USB Device Mode provides an easy way to connect your laptop directly to a Jetson with minimal cabling.</p>

    <img src="/images/workshops/usb-device-mode_Type-A.png" alt="USB Device Mode connection diagram" class="tutorial-img" style="max-width: 100%;" />

    <p>Follow these steps:</p>
    <ol>
      <li><p>Take the Jetson developer kit out of the box and connect the bundled power supply.</p></li>
      <li><p>Find a USB cable to connect the Jetson to your PC (USB-C end goes into the Jetson).</p></li>
      <li><p>You should see a "L4T-README" drive automatically mounted on your PC.</p></li>
      <li>
        <p>Open a terminal on your PC and SSH into the Jetson:</p>
        <div class="admonition info">
          <p class="admonition-title">🔰 What is SSH?</p>
          <p>SSH (Secure Shell) lets you remotely control the Jetson from your own laptop's terminal — as if you were typing directly on the Jetson. No monitor or keyboard needs to be plugged into the Jetson itself.</p>
        </div>
        <p>On <strong>Windows</strong>, open <strong>PowerShell</strong> or <strong>Command Prompt</strong>. On <strong>Mac/Linux</strong>, open <strong>Terminal</strong>. Then type:</p>
        <pre style="background: #1e293b; color: #e2e8f0; padding: 1rem; border-radius: 8px;"><code>{"ssh USERNAME@192.168.55.1"}</code></pre>
        <div class="admonition note">
          <p class="admonition-title">📘 Note</p>
          <p>Replace <code>USERNAME</code> with the username set on the Jetson. Check with the person or organization who provided the hardware for the username and password. You will be prompted for the password after running the command.</p>
          <p>The address <code>192.168.55.1</code> is a fixed IP that the Jetson assigns itself over the USB cable — you do not need to look it up.</p>
        </div>
      </li>
    </ol>

    <details class="nv-details">
      <summary>How USB Device Mode works</summary>
      <div class="nv-details-content">
        <p>When Jetson is connected to a PC, it acts as a USB Composite Device that presents several USB device classes:</p>
        <ul>
          <li><strong>USB Ethernet</strong> - provides a virtual NIC for SSH access</li>
          <li><strong>USB Mass Storage</strong> - lets the PC mount the "L4T-README" drive</li>
          <li><strong>USB Serial</strong> - provides serial console access</li>
        </ul>
        <p>When connected to a Windows PC, "Device Manager" in "Devices by connection" view shows something like the following:</p>
        <img src="/images/workshops/windows_device_manager.png" alt="Windows Device Manager showing USB device mode" class="tutorial-img" style="max-width: 480px;" />
      </div>
    </details>

    <p>You can also get Jetson on a Wi-Fi network to have Internet access and allow your team members to simultaneously access Jetson.</p>
  </div>

  <div class="nv-tab-panel">
    <p>If you have a PC monitor, DisplayPort cable, USB keyboard, and mouse, you can use Jetson as a standalone computer and do all the work on it.</p>
  </div>
</Tabs>


## Network Connection

After you've connected to the Jetson via USB (above), you'll want to connect it to a network so it has **internet access** (for downloading models, packages, etc.) and so **multiple team members** can SSH into it at the same time.

<div class="admonition info">
  <p class="admonition-title">🔰 Why do I need this?</p>
  <p>The USB connection only lets <strong>one PC</strong> talk to the Jetson and does <strong>not</strong> provide internet. Connecting to Wi-Fi or Ethernet gives the Jetson its own IP address on the local network, enabling internet access and multi-user SSH.</p>
</div>

<Tabs labels={["Wi-Fi", "Ethernet (wired)"]}>
  <div class="nv-tab-panel active">
    <p>While SSH'd into the Jetson via USB, run this command to connect to Wi-Fi:</p>
    <pre style="background: #1e293b; color: #e2e8f0; padding: 1rem; border-radius: 8px;"><code>{"sudo nmcli device wifi connect WIFI_SSID password WIFI_PASSWORD"}</code></pre>
    <p>Replace <code>WIFI_SSID</code> with your Wi-Fi network name and <code>WIFI_PASSWORD</code> with the password.</p>
  </div>

  <div class="nv-tab-panel">
    <p>If an Ethernet cable is available, simply plug it into the Ethernet port on the Jetson. Most networks will automatically assign an IP address via DHCP — no extra commands needed.</p>
  </div>
</Tabs>

### Finding the Jetson's IP Address

Once connected to Wi-Fi or Ethernet, find the Jetson's new IP address by running this on the Jetson (via your existing USB SSH session):

<pre style="background: #1e293b; color: #e2e8f0; padding: 1rem; border-radius: 8px;"><code>{"hostname -I"}</code></pre>

This prints the IP address(es) assigned to the Jetson. Look for an address like `192.168.x.x` or `10.x.x.x` (ignore `192.168.55.1` — that's the USB connection).

### SSH over the Network

Once the Jetson is on your Wi-Fi or Ethernet network, **you no longer need the USB cable**. Any team member on the **same network** can SSH into the Jetson directly from their own laptop:

<pre style="background: #1e293b; color: #e2e8f0; padding: 1rem; border-radius: 8px;"><code>{"ssh USERNAME@JETSON_IP"}</code></pre>

Replace `USERNAME` with your Jetson's username and `JETSON_IP` with the address you found above (e.g. `ssh jetson@192.168.1.42`).

<div class="admonition info">
  <p class="admonition-title">🔰 No USB cable required</p>
  <p>The USB cable was only needed for the <strong>initial setup</strong> — to connect the Jetson to Wi-Fi or Ethernet. Once the Jetson has a network IP address, you can <strong>unplug the USB cable</strong> and SSH over the network from any laptop. Multiple team members can connect at the same time, each from their own machine.</p>
</div>

<div class="admonition tip">
  <p class="admonition-title">💡 Tip</p>
  <p>Sometimes, the Wi-Fi network policy blocks device-to-device connections (like SSH).</p>
  <p>USB Device Mode still lets you connect via the USB cable, but limits access to one PC. If your team needs multiple PCs to access Jetson simultaneously but Wi-Fi blocks SSH, bring a <strong>portable Wi-Fi router</strong> to create a local network, or use an <strong>Ethernet cable</strong> to a shared switch/router.</p>
</div>

### Use VS Code or Cursor for Remote Development

Instead of working in a plain terminal, you can connect **VS Code** or **Cursor** directly to the Jetson over SSH. This gives you a full IDE experience — file browsing, code editing with syntax highlighting, integrated terminal, and extensions — all running on your laptop while the code executes on the Jetson.

<ol>
  <li>
    <p>Install the <strong>Remote - SSH</strong> extension:</p>
    <ul>
      <li><strong>VS Code</strong>: Open Extensions (<code>Ctrl+Shift+X</code>), search for <strong>"Remote - SSH"</strong> by Microsoft, and install it.</li>
      <li><strong>Cursor</strong>: The Remote - SSH extension is built-in — no installation needed.</li>
    </ul>
    <img src="/images/workshops/vscode-remote-ssh-extension.png" alt="Remote - SSH extension in VS Code marketplace" class="tutorial-img" style="max-width: 100%; margin-top: 0.75rem;" />
  </li>
  <li>
    <p>Open the Command Palette (<code>Ctrl+Shift+P</code> / <code>Cmd+Shift+P</code> on Mac) and select <strong>"Remote-SSH: Connect to Host..."</strong></p>
    <p>Enter the SSH connection string — for example: <code>jetson@192.168.1.42</code></p>
    <pre style="background: #1e293b; color: #e2e8f0; padding: 1rem; border-radius: 8px;"><code>{"USERNAME@JETSON_IP"}</code></pre>
  </li>
  <li>
    <p>When prompted, select <strong>Linux</strong> as the platform for the remote host:</p>
    <img src="/images/workshops/vscode-remote-ssh-platform-select.png" alt="Select Linux as the remote host platform" class="tutorial-img" style="max-width: 520px; margin-top: 0.75rem;" />
  </li>
  <li>
    <p>Enter the password when prompted:</p>
    <img src="/images/workshops/vscode-remote-ssh-password.png" alt="Password prompt for SSH connection" class="tutorial-img" style="max-width: 520px; margin-top: 0.75rem;" />
    <p>The editor will connect and install a lightweight server on the Jetson automatically.</p>
  </li>
  <li>
    <p>Once connected, you'll see <strong>"SSH: &lt;IP address&gt;"</strong> in the bottom-left corner. Use <strong>Open Folder</strong> to browse and edit files on the Jetson, and open the integrated terminal (<code>Ctrl+`</code>) to run commands — all from the comfort of your laptop's IDE.</p>
    <img src="/images/workshops/vscode-remote-ssh-connected.png" alt="VS Code connected to Jetson via SSH showing Open Folder and SSH indicator" class="tutorial-img" style="max-width: 420px; margin-top: 0.75rem;" />
  </li>
</ol>

<div class="admonition tip">
  <p class="admonition-title">💡 Tip — Save your SSH config for one-click access</p>
  <p>To avoid typing the IP address every time, add the Jetson to your SSH config file (<code>~/.ssh/config</code>):</p>
  <pre style="background: #1e293b; color: #e2e8f0; padding: 1rem; border-radius: 8px;"><code>{"Host jetson\n    HostName 192.168.1.42\n    User jetson"}</code></pre>
  <p>Replace the IP and username with your values. After this, you can simply select <strong>"jetson"</strong> from the host list in VS Code/Cursor — or type <code>ssh jetson</code> in a terminal.</p>
</div>

## Troubleshooting

<details class="nv-details">
  <summary>Cannot SSH into Jetson after connecting USB-C cable</summary>
  <div class="nv-details-content">
    <ul>
      <li>Check if the green LED near the USB-C port is lit (power indicator). If not, verify the DC power supply connection to the wall outlet and the Jetson carrier board.</li>
      <li>Check if the "L4T-README" drive appears on your PC. If not, try a different USB-C cable or check connections on both ends.</li>
    </ul>
  </div>
</details>

<details class="nv-details">
  <summary>Cannot log in with provided credentials</summary>
  <div class="nv-details-content">
    <ul>
      <li>Double-check the username and password you typed.</li>
      <li>Someone may have changed the password of the default user. If you cannot find the updated password, you may need to reflash - see the <a href="/tutorials/initial-setup-jetson-orin-nano">Initial Setup Guide</a>.</li>
    </ul>
  </div>
</details>

<details class="nv-details">
  <summary>Jetson seems slow</summary>
  <div class="nv-details-content">
    <p>Check your power mode:</p>
    <pre style="background: #1e293b; color: #e2e8f0; padding: 1rem; border-radius: 8px;"><code>{"nvpmodel -q"}</code></pre>
    <p>Install <a href="https://github.com/rbonghi/jetson_stats">jtop (jetson-stats)</a> to monitor resource utilization:</p>
    <pre style="background: #1e293b; color: #e2e8f0; padding: 1rem; border-radius: 8px;"><code>{"sudo apt install -y python3-pip\nsudo pip3 install -U jetson-stats"}</code></pre>
    <p>The <code>CTRL</code> tab in jtop lets you change the power mode under <code>NVP modes</code>.</p>
  </div>
</details>

<details class="nv-details">
  <summary>How to check Jetson resource utilization in real-time?</summary>
  <div class="nv-details-content">
    <p>Install <a href="https://github.com/rbonghi/jetson_stats">jtop</a>:</p>
    <pre style="background: #1e293b; color: #e2e8f0; padding: 1rem; border-radius: 8px;"><code>{"sudo apt install -y python3-pip\nsudo pip3 install -U jetson-stats"}</code></pre>
  </div>
</details>

<details class="nv-details">
  <summary>How to power Jetson with a battery?</summary>
  <div class="nv-details-content">
    <p>Use a USB power bank that supports 12V output via Power Delivery (PD) with a USB PD to DC power jack adapter cable.</p>
  </div>
</details>
